Autonomous Floating Garden

Floating gardening system designed for sustainable freshwater-based food production

January 2025 - April 2025 Cynthia Hsiao

The Autonomous Floating Garden (AFG) is a floating gardening system designed for individuals and communities with access to freshwater who want to grow their own produce. Created in response to rising food costs and limited growing space, the system provides an alternative way to support small-scale, sustainable food production.

Designed to float securely on lakes or ponds, the AFG allows users to cultivate plants without requiring traditional land-based gardens. An integrated automated irrigation system with moisture sensing helps maintain consistent hydration while minimizing maintenance.

The project focuses on accessibility, sustainability, and efficient design, offering a practical solution to improve food resilience for waterfront homeowners, rural communities, and freshwater-based businesses.

Flotation Calculations

To determine the required flotation for the Autonomous Floating Garden, we calculated the buoyant force provided by 2L bottles using the buoyancy equation and evaluated configurations ranging from 1 to 15 bottles.

We then calculated the total mass of the system, including the wooden structure, electronic components, bottles, and saturated soil, to estimate the maximum operating load. By comparing the total system weight to the buoyant force generated by different numbers of bottles, we found that a minimum of 7 bottles was required to achieve positive buoyancy.

To improve reliability and account for additional load, we selected 10 bottles, providing support for approximately 7.6 kg of additional mass and achieving a factor of safety of 1.8. This ensured the AFG would remain safely buoyant while allowing height adjustments through an anchor rope system.

Prototype Final CAD Design

This CAD design was developed to address the issue of the two main components becoming detached during operation. To ensure a secure and stable connection, we incorporated a slot-and-indent system that allows the soil insert to fit snugly into the wooden base, reducing movement and improving overall structural integrity.

The soil insert was designed to be constructed from aluminum due to its lightweight properties, durability, and resistance to corrosion. The overall dimensions of the system are 60 cm in length, 45 cm in width, and 15 cm in height.

The design accommodates two 20 cm × 20 cm plant pots and includes space for two solar panels. For optimal balance and weight distribution, the solar panels are positioned on opposing ends of the structure. The pump is strategically mounted on the side and integrated into a pre-designed opening to ensure secure placement and efficient operation.
